Alpöge's counterexample to the Jacobian conjecture in dimension three (announced July 19, 2026) settled an 87 year old question, and its consequences are still being worked out in real time. This post collects three explicit specimens I have built on top of the map in the days since, together with a structural fact about the map itself. Every computational claim below is certified twice: by exact computer algebra, and by machine-checked Lean 4 / mathlib proofs with no sorry and a clean axiom audit. Full write-up: PDF · sympy certificate · Lean proofs and scripts.

The map

With \(u = 1 + x_1 x_2\), Alpöge's map \(F = (F_1, F_2, F_3)\colon \mathbb{C}^3 \to \mathbb{C}^3\) is

$$ \begin{aligned} F_1 &= u^3 x_3 + x_2^2\,u\,(4 + 3 x_1 x_2), \\ F_2 &= x_2 + 3 x_1 u^2 x_3 + 3 x_1 x_2^2 (4 + 3 x_1 x_2), \\ F_3 &= 2 x_1 - 3 x_1^2 x_2 - x_1^3 x_3, \end{aligned} $$

with \(\det J = -2\) identically and the explicit collision

$$ F(0, 0, -\tfrac{1}{4}) = F(1, -\tfrac{3}{2}, \tfrac{13}{2}) = F(-1, \tfrac{3}{2}, \tfrac{13}{2}) = (-\tfrac{1}{4}, 0, 0). $$

Because the determinant is a unit, \(N = -\tfrac{1}{2}\,\mathrm{adj}\,J = J^{-1}\) is a matrix of polynomials. Everything below flows from two certified families of identities: \(JN = NJ = I_3\), and the flatness identities \(\sum_k (N_{ki}\,\partial_k N_{lj} - N_{kj}\,\partial_k N_{li}) = 0\).

1. The Weyl algebra endomorphism

The cotangent lift \(\varphi(\hat{x}_i) = F_i\), \(\varphi(\partial_i) = \sum_k N_{ki}\,\partial_k\) extends to an injective, non-surjective endomorphism of the third Weyl algebra \(A_3\), disproving the Dixmier conjecture \(\mathrm{DC}_3\). Since the lifted operators have order at most one, no quantum corrections arise, and well-definedness is equivalent to the two identity families above. An independent note by Pickhardt and an AI research agent, dated July 19, carries out this transfer too, with an elegant proof that the pullback preserves differential order exactly; what my treatment adds on this front is the machine-checked certification. The two specimens that follow, and the rigidity result, appear only here.

2. The symplectic and Poisson counterexamples on \(\mathbb{C}^6\)

The classical shadow of \(\varphi\) is the cotangent lift

$$ \Phi\colon \mathbb{C}^6 \to \mathbb{C}^6, \qquad \Phi(x, y) = \bigl(F(x),\; N(x)^{\mathsf{T}} y\bigr), $$

on \(\mathbb{C}^6 = T^*\mathbb{C}^3\). It preserves the canonical Poisson bracket, satisfies \(\det J\Phi = 1\) identically, and is not injective: since \(N(x)\) is invertible at every point, the collision lifts to \(\Phi(a, y_a) = \Phi(b, y_b)\) for any \(y_a\) with \(y_b = (N(b)^{\mathsf{T}})^{-1} N(a)^{\mathsf{T}} y_a\). The pullback \(\Phi^*\) is therefore a non-invertible Poisson endomorphism, an explicit counterexample to the Poisson conjecture \(\mathrm{PC}_3\), and \(\Phi\) is, to my knowledge, the first symplectic Keller counterexample. A determinant-one counterexample on \(\mathbb{C}^3\) is trivial from \(F\) by a linear volume correction; the point of \(\Phi\) is the symplectic structure, which no map on odd-dimensional \(\mathbb{C}^3\) can carry.

3. The contact counterexample on \(\mathbb{C}^7\)

\(\Phi\) preserves more than the symplectic form. Writing \(\lambda = \sum_i y_i\,dx_i\) for the Liouville form,

$$ \Phi^*\lambda = \sum_i (N^{\mathsf{T}}y)_i\, dF_i = \sum_k \bigl((NJ)^{\mathsf{T}}y\bigr)_k\, dx_k = \lambda, $$

exactly, because \(NJ = I_3\). Now take \(\mathbb{C}^7\) with coordinates \((x, y, z)\) and the standard contact form \(\alpha = dz - \lambda\). As far as I can tell, no contact analogue of the Jacobian conjecture appears in the literature, so let me state the natural question first: is every polynomial endomorphism of \(\mathbb{C}^{2n+1}\) preserving \(\alpha\) exactly an automorphism? The answer is no for every \(n \ge 3\): the contactization

$$ \Psi(x, y, z) = \bigl(F(x),\; N(x)^{\mathsf{T}} y,\; z\bigr) $$

satisfies \(\Psi^*\alpha = dz - \Phi^*\lambda = \alpha\) on the nose, has \(\det J\Psi = 1\) identically, and inherits the collision. This is, to my knowledge, the first non-invertible polynomial strict contactomorphism of affine space. The frontier cases are open: \(\mathbb{C}^5\) would require a symplectic counterexample in two degrees of freedom, which is \(\mathrm{PC}_2\) territory.

4. Rigidity: locally, there is exactly one counterexample

A structural fact about \(F\) itself: it admits no infinitesimal deformations beyond composition with automorphisms. Every first-order Keller deformation of a map with constant Jacobian is of the form \(A \circ F + J_F \cdot B\) with \(\mathrm{div}\,A = \mathrm{div}\,B = 0\), and pushing the obstruction calculus to fifth order (in exact arithmetic, independently at two primes) kills every direction outside the affine-composition orbit. In particular the one direction that survives fourth order, \(B = y\,(x/3, -2y/3, z)\), dies at order five. So within its degree range the counterexample is formally rigid modulo symmetries: locally, Alpöge's map is the only one. Scripts for this computation are in the repository; this result is certified by exact modular computation rather than Lean.

Certification

The sympy script verifies, in exact rational arithmetic: \(\det J = -2\), \(JN = I_3\), the nine flatness identities, bracket preservation and \(\det J\Phi = 1\) for \(\Phi\), and the Liouville identity \(\Phi^*\lambda = \lambda\) behind the contact theorem. The Lean 4 / mathlib development proves the Keller property with the Jacobian entries defined as formal partial derivatives, the three-point collision, the inverse identities \(J \cdot \mathrm{adj}\,J = \mathrm{adj}\,J \cdot J = -2 I_3\), all 27 flatness cases, and the block determinant certifying \(\det J\Phi = 1\); every theorem depends only on the three standard axioms.

What remains

\(\mathrm{DC}_1\), \(\mathrm{DC}_2\), \(\mathrm{PC}_1\), \(\mathrm{PC}_2\) are open, and \(\mathrm{PC}_1\) is essentially the plane Jacobian conjecture, which survives everything above. Zheglov (arXiv:2410.06959) proposes a proof of \(\mathrm{DC}_1\). If it holds, a hypothetical plane counterexample can never quantize to an endomorphism of \(A_1\), whereas in dimension three quantization succeeds with no corrections at all. Where exactly the passage from geometry to operators stops working in low dimension is, I think, the most interesting question these specimens raise.
